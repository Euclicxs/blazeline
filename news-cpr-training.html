Community CPR Training Session

Free certification class open to all community members

The Blazeline Fire Department is excited to announce a free Community CPR Training Session scheduled for Saturday, January 20th, 2024. This hands-on training program is designed to equip community members with life-saving skills that can make a critical difference in emergency situations.

Event Details

Date: Saturday, January 20th, 2024

Time: 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Location: Blazeline Fire Station, 123 Fire Station Lane

Cost: Free (Registration required)

Age Requirement: 16 years and older

What You'll Learn

This comprehensive training session will cover essential CPR techniques and emergency response procedures:

  • Adult, child, and infant CPR techniques
  • Automated External Defibrillator (AED) operation
  • Choking relief procedures
  • Recognition of cardiac arrest
  • Emergency response protocols
  • Hands-only CPR techniques

"CPR can double or even triple a person's chance of survival in cardiac arrest situations. Every member of our community should have these life-saving skills."

â€” Paramedic David Thompson, Training Coordinator

Training Format

The session will include both theoretical instruction and practical hands-on training using CPR mannequins. Participants will have the opportunity to practice techniques under the guidance of our certified instructors. The training follows American Heart Association guidelines and participants will receive a certification of completion.

Registration Information

Space is limited to ensure personalized instruction and adequate practice time. Interested participants must register in advance by:

  1. Calling our training hotline at (555) 012-3456
  2. Emailing training@blazelinefd.org
  3. Registering online through our website

What to Bring

Participants should bring:

  • Comfortable clothing suitable for hands-on practice
  • Water bottle and light snacks
  • Notebook and pen for taking notes
  • Valid photo ID for certification purposes

Additional Sessions

Due to expected high demand, we plan to offer additional CPR training sessions throughout the year. If you cannot attend this session, please let us know and we will notify you about future training opportunities.

Community Impact

By training more community members in CPR, we create a network of potential life-savers throughout Blazeline. Statistics show that communities with higher rates of CPR training have significantly better outcomes for cardiac arrest victims.

Don't miss this opportunity to learn skills that could save a life. Whether you're a parent, teacher, coach, or simply a concerned community member, this training will prepare you to respond effectively in emergency situations.
