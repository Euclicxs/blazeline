Winter Heating Safety Tips

Important safety reminders for keeping your home warm and safe during the winter months

As winter approaches and temperatures drop, the Blazeline Fire Department wants to ensure our community stays warm and safe. Heating equipment is a leading cause of home fires during winter months, but with proper precautions, you can significantly reduce the risk.

Space Heater Safety

Space heaters are a common source of supplemental heat, but they require careful attention to safety:

  • Keep 3 feet of clearance: Maintain at least 3 feet of space between heaters and anything that can burn, including curtains, furniture, and bedding.
  • Never leave unattended: Turn off space heaters when leaving the room or going to sleep.
  • Use certified heaters only: Choose models with automatic shut-off features and safety certifications.
  • Place on level surfaces: Never place heaters on furniture or uneven surfaces where they could tip over.
  • Plug directly into outlets: Avoid using extension cords or power strips with space heaters.

Furnace and Heating System Maintenance

Regular maintenance of your heating system is crucial for both safety and efficiency:

  • Annual professional inspection: Have your furnace inspected by a qualified technician before each heating season.
  • Change filters regularly: Replace furnace filters according to manufacturer recommendations.
  • Check for gas leaks: If you smell gas, leave immediately and call 911 from a safe location.
  • Clear vents and chimneys: Ensure all vents are clear of debris and obstructions.
  • Install carbon monoxide detectors: Place detectors near sleeping areas and test them monthly.

Fireplace and Wood Stove Safety

Traditional heating methods require special attention to safety:

  • Annual chimney cleaning: Have chimneys inspected and cleaned by a professional annually.
  • Use proper fuel: Burn only seasoned hardwood in fireplaces and wood stoves.
  • Install spark screens: Use metal mesh screens to prevent sparks from escaping.
  • Dispose of ashes safely: Place ashes in metal containers and store away from the house.
  • Keep combustibles away: Maintain at least 3 feet of clearance around heating appliances.

  • Never use outdoor heating equipment indoors
  • Don't run vehicles in attached garages
  • If CO alarm sounds, leave immediately and call 911

Emergency Preparedness

Be prepared for heating-related emergencies:

  • Create an escape plan: Ensure all family members know two ways out of every room.
  • Practice fire drills: Conduct regular fire drills, especially during heating season.
  • Keep fire extinguishers handy: Place extinguishers in key areas and know how to use them.
  • Have emergency contacts ready: Post emergency numbers where everyone can find them.
  • Check smoke alarms: Test monthly and replace batteries as needed.

Energy Efficiency Tips

Stay warm while keeping energy costs down:

  • Seal drafts around windows and doors
  • Use programmable thermostats to reduce heating when away
  • Open curtains during sunny days to let natural heat in
  • Close curtains at night to retain heat
  • Ensure proper insulation in attics and walls

When to Seek Help

Contact the Blazeline Fire Department if you:

  • Smell gas or suspect a gas leak
  • Have questions about heating safety
  • Need smoke or CO detector installation assistance
  • Want a home safety inspection
  • Experience any heating equipment malfunctions

Remember, taking time to ensure your heating systems are safe and properly maintained can prevent fires and protect your family throughout the winter season. Stay warm, stay safe, and don't hesitate to reach out with any safety concerns.
